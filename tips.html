🏆 Top 10 Sweeping Tips
01

Create a Dedicated Sweeping Email Address

Set up a free Gmail or Yahoo account just for sweepstakes entries. This keeps your personal inbox clean and ensures you never miss a winner notification. Check it daily — many sweepstakes give winners only 3–5 days to respond before selecting another winner!

02

Enter Daily-Entry Sweepstakes Every Single Day

Daily-entry sweepstakes give you multiple chances to win. If you enter 30 times over a month versus once, your odds improve dramatically. Set a morning alarm and make it a 5-minute routine. Bookmark your favorites or use a sweepstakes tracker app.

03

Focus on Sweepstakes With Fewer Entries

A $500 local grocery store sweepstakes open only to residents of your county may have 200 entries. A national $50,000 contest from a major brand may have 5 million. Your odds in the local one are dramatically better. Don't only chase the big prizes!

04

Always Read the Official Rules Before Entering

The official rules tell you the exact entry dates, who is eligible, what states can enter, how the winner is selected, and what taxes or other obligations come with winning. Never assume anything — the rules are the final word.

05

Keep a Sweeping Log or Spreadsheet

Track what you enter, when you entered, the entry deadline, and the prize. Not only does this prevent double-entering, but it helps you identify which sweepstakes to re-enter and lets you know when to expect winner announcements.

06

Enter Mail-In Sweepstakes — Most People Don't

Many sweepstakes still accept AMOE (Alternative Method of Entry) via mail. Very few people send physical entries. For a 3¢ stamp, you may have odds far better than the thousands of online entrants. Look for "no purchase necessary" mail-in options in the official rules.

07

Play Instant Win Games During Off-Peak Hours

Instant win games have a set number of winning moments — times when the next person to play wins. There's no proven "best time," but playing during low-traffic hours (early morning, late night) means less competition for those winning moments.

08

Use Auto-Fill to Speed Up Entry

Your browser's autofill feature or a password manager can fill out entry forms in seconds. This is especially valuable for daily-entry sweepstakes where you fill out the same form every day. Test your autofill and keep your saved data updated.

09

Never Pay to Enter or "Win"

Legitimate sweepstakes are always free to enter. If you're asked to pay a fee to claim a prize, pay for shipping on a prize, or provide payment info to "receive" winnings — it's a scam. The FTC has strict rules: no purchase may be necessary to enter or win.

10

Don't Give Up — Winners Are Real People Like You

Read our Winners Circle — these are regular people who simply kept entering. Most winners entered for months before their first win. Consistency over time is the single biggest factor. Set realistic expectations, enjoy the process, and you'll win eventually.

⚠️ Sweepstakes Scam Warning Signs

  • You're asked to pay any fee to enter or claim your prize
  • You "won" a sweepstakes you never entered
  • You must provide your Social Security Number or bank account info to collect
  • The prize seems impossibly large and requires no entry effort
  • Contact comes via unsolicited text message or social media DM
  • The sponsor has no official website or verifiable contact information
❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Do I really have a chance of winning? +
Yes! Sweepstakes winners are selected randomly, which means everyone has a real mathematical chance. That said, your odds depend heavily on how many total entries are received. Entering more sweepstakes, entering daily when allowed, and focusing on less-popular contests all improve your chances. Our Winners Circle proves real people win every month.
Is it safe to enter my personal information? +
For legitimate sweepstakes from recognizable sponsors, yes. Companies use this info to contact winners and for optional marketing. Always use a dedicated sweeping email address. Never provide your Social Security number, full bank account info, or payment info to enter a sweepstakes — that's a scam. All sweepstakes on this site have been reviewed for legitimacy.
Do I have to pay taxes on prizes I win? +
In the United States, sweepstakes prizes are generally considered taxable income by the IRS. Cash prizes are straightforward — the fair market value is taxable. Non-cash prizes (cars, vacations, etc.) are taxed at their fair market value. Sponsors must report prizes over $600 to the IRS. Before accepting a large prize, consult a tax professional to understand your obligations.
What's the difference between a sweepstakes and a contest? +
A sweepstakes is a game of chance — winners are selected randomly from all eligible entries. Skill is not a factor. A contest is a game of skill — winners are judged on some merit like writing, photography, or video. Both must be free to enter (legally). Lotteries require payment and are regulated differently by each state.
How do I know if I've won? +
Most sponsors notify winners by email, so keeping an eye on your sweeping inbox is crucial. Some call by phone. Always check your spam folder — winner emails sometimes get filtered. Response deadlines are typically 3–10 days, after which an alternate winner is chosen. Some sweepstakes post winners publicly on their website or announce via social media.
Can I enter sweepstakes if I live outside the US? +
Most sweepstakes listed on this site are open to US residents only (due to legal and tax regulations). Some are open to US and Canada. A small number are international. Always check the official rules for geographic eligibility. We note eligibility requirements on each listing — look for "US 18+" or "US, Canada" in the entry details.
Is MySweepstakesHub free to use? +
Yes, 100% free. We list sweepstakes as a community resource. We earn revenue from advertising on the site. We will never charge you to view or access sweepstakes listings, and we will never ask for payment to enter any contest — that's illegal. Our daily email newsletter is also completely free.
